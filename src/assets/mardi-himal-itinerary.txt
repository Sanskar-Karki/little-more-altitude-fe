Mardi Himal Trek Itinerary

Overview:
The trek begins with a scenic drive from Pokhara to Sidding, where the trail starts ascending through peaceful forests and traditional villages. From Sidding to Low Camp, you walk through rhododendron and oak forests, gradually gaining altitude while enjoying glimpses of the Annapurna range.

The trail continues to High Camp, surrounded by towering peaks and serene alpine landscapes. From High Camp, trekkers make an early morning hike to the View Point to witness a breathtaking sunrise over Machapuchare (Fishtail), Hiunchuli, and Annapurna South, creating a truly unforgettable Himalayan moment.

The return journey descends through Forest Camp to Kalimati, passing lush forests and tranquil valleys where many different species of Himalayan birds can be spotted, making the trek a paradise for nature and bird enthusiasts. From Kalimati, trekkers are driven back to Pokhara, completing a 5–7-day adventure filled with majestic mountain views, peaceful trails, and authentic Himalayan culture.

Trip Highlights & Info:
Maximum Altitude: 4,500m – Mardi Himal Viewpoint
Best Season: Spring and Autumn
Duration: 5–7 days
Daily Activity: 4–6 hours on average

Itinerary:
Day 1: Arrival in Kathmandu and shift to hotel
Day 2: Kathmandu → Pokhara (822m)
A scenic drive through river valleys and rolling hills brings you to Pokhara, the gateway to the Annapurna region, where mountain views set the tone for the adventure ahead.
Day 3: Pokhara → Siddhing (1,700m) → Low Camp (2,970m)
After a drive to Siddhing, the trek begins through terraced farmland and dense forests, gradually climbing to Low Camp surrounded by peaceful mountain landscapes.
Day 4: Low Camp (2,970m) → High Camp (3,580m)
Today’s trek follows a ridge trail through rhododendron forests, opening up to breathtaking views of Machhapuchhre, Annapurna South, and the surrounding peaks as you reach High Camp.
Day 5: High Camp → Viewpoint (4,500m) → Rest Camp (2,600m)
An early morning hike leads to the Mardi Himal Viewpoint for a spectacular sunrise over the Himalayas. After returning to High Camp for breakfast, the trail descends through alpine terrain to Rest Camp.
Day 6: Rest Camp → Kalimati (1,800m) via Forest Camp → Pokhara
The final day takes you through lush forests rich in birdlife, passing Forest Camp before reaching Kalimati, from where we drive back to Pokhara.
Day 7: Pokhara to Kathmandu drive
Day 8: Departure from Kathmandu to your home country
